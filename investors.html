The Company will establish wholly owned subsidiaries to negotiate and own reinsurance, profit-sharing and ownership treaties for life insurance and other financial services products offered through various providers in the United States and Canada.

Under a reinsurance agreement, certain insurance risks are transferred, or ceded, from one insurance company to another to share the risk. Ceding refers to the use of reinsurance to transfer some or all, of the risks associated with one or more insurance policies from one insurance company to another. These risks may include mortality, persistency, investment and expense.

The ceding insurance company generally retains responsibility for the payment of all claims, surrender values, commissions and expenses involved in issuing and maintaining the policies reinsured. In addition, the ceding insurance company administers the reinsurance contracts and provides the reinsurance company with information on a periodic basis regarding premiums, reserves, benefits and settlement amounts for the policies ceded to and reinsured by the reinsurance company.
